
=== Suggested Questions on the Lesson

Younger Children: From what book of the Bible is our lesson for today? Judges

Older Children: Who were the Judges? temporary local leaders

Older Children: In what way were the Judges different from Moses and Joshua as leaders? not great leaders of entire people

Older Children: What was the trouble with the people in the period of the Judges? forgot God

Younger Children: Which Judge is our lesson about today? Gideon

Older Children: From what tribe did he come? Manasseh

Younger Children: What enemy was attacking Israel? Midian (also Amalek)

Older Children: What was the first thing Gideon did after he was called? humbled himself

Older Children: What assurance did he ask of the Lord? fleece

Older Children: From what tribes did he gather his army? Asher, Zebulun, Naphtali, Manasseh

Younger Children: Why did the army have to be reduced in numbers? God to give victory

Younger Children: Who were first sent home? fearful

Younger Children: What was the second test? water drinking

Younger Children: How large was the army finally? three hundred

Younger Children: What did they have instead of weapons? lamps, pitchers, trumpets

Older Children: How was the attack made? surrounded enemy, broke jars, shouted

Older Children: What was the result? confusion, victory

Younger Teens: What do the Judges represent? particular truths needed in time of trouble

Older Teens: What do you think are the important lessons taught in this story? trust the Lord, get rid of fearful thoughts, be prepared to face ridicule, ask the Lord what is right
