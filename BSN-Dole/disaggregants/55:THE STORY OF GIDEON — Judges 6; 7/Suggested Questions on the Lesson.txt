
=== Suggested Questions on the Lesson

*Q:* (Younger Children): From what book of the Bible is our lesson for today? +
*A:* Judges

*Q:* (Older Children): Who were the Judges? +
*A:* temporary local leaders

*Q:* (Older Children): In what way were the Judges different from Moses and Joshua as leaders? +
*A:* not great leaders of entire people

*Q:* (Older Children): What was the trouble with the people in the period of the Judges? +
*A:* forgot God

*Q:* (Younger Children): Which Judge is our lesson about today? +
*A:* Gideon

*Q:* (Older Children): From what tribe did he come? +
*A:* Manasseh

*Q:* (Younger Children): What enemy was attacking Israel? +
*A:* Midian (also Amalek)

*Q:* (Older Children): What was the first thing Gideon did after he was called? +
*A:* humbled himself

*Q:* (Older Children): What assurance did he ask of the Lord? +
*A:* fleece

*Q:* (Older Children): From what tribes did he gather his army? +
*A:* Asher, Zebulun, Naphtali, Manasseh

*Q:* (Younger Children): Why did the army have to be reduced in numbers? +
*A:* God to give victory

*Q:* (Younger Children): Who were first sent home? +
*A:* fearful

*Q:* (Younger Children): What was the second test? +
*A:* water drinking

*Q:* (Younger Children): How large was the army finally? +
*A:* three hundred

*Q:* (Younger Children): What did they have instead of weapons? +
*A:* lamps, pitchers, trumpets

*Q:* (Older Children): How was the attack made? +
*A:* surrounded enemy, broke jars, shouted

*Q:* (Older Children): What was the result? +
*A:* confusion, victory

*Q:* (Younger Teens): What do the Judges represent? +
*A:* particular truths needed in time of trouble

*Q:* (Older Teens): What do you think are the important lessons taught in this story? +
*A:* trust the Lord, get rid of fearful thoughts, be prepared to face ridicule, ask the Lord what is right
