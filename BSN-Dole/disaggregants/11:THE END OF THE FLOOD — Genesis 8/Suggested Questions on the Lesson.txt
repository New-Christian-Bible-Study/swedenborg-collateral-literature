
=== Suggested Questions on the Lesson

Younger Children: After men were created, where were they placed? Eden

Younger Teens: Why were they not satisfied? wanted independence

Younger Teens: How did the Lord meet their new need? showed them how to use self-hood constructively

Younger Children: Why couldn't men always live in the beautiful Garden of Eden? disobeyed

Older Children: Why did they become worse as they went on? one bad deed led to another

Older Children: What finally happened to most of them? destroyed by own evil

Younger Children: Who was saved? Noah

Younger Children: How was Noah saved? ark

Younger Children: How long did the rain last? forty days

Younger Children: Where was the window which Noah opened when the rain stopped? top

Younger Children: What two birds did he send out to see if the waters had gone down? raven, dove

Younger Children: What did the raven do? nothing

Younger Children: How many times did Noah send out the dove? three

Younger Children: What happened the first time? came back

Younger Children: What did the dove bring back the second time? olive leaf

Older Children: What happened the third time? did not return

Older Children: What was the first thing Noah did when he came out of the ark? built altar

Older Children: What did the Lord promise? no more floods

Intermediate. What is meant by the flood? false ideas

Younger Teens: What are meant by the raven and the dove? false and true thoughts

Older Teens: To what does the olive leaf correspond? knowledge of how to serve Lord

Older Teens: What change did the Lord make in man's constitution at the beginning of the Ancient Church? Separated will and understanding; people were no longer led by perception, but now were led by conscience

Older Children: What does mythology call the civilization described by Noah? Silver Age

Older Children: What does Swedenborg call it? Ancient Church
