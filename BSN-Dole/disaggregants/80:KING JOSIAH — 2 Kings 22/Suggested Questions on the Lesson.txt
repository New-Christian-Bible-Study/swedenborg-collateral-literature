
=== Suggested Questions on the Lesson

Older Children: What part of the divided kingdom remained loyal to the line of David? Judah

Younger Children: Were all the kings of Judah bad like those of Israel? no

Younger Children: What king is our lesson about today? Josiah

Younger Children: How old was he when he became king? eight

Older Children: What was the first of his good works? repaired temple

Older Children: How were the repairs of the temple paid for? contributions

Younger Children: What was found when the temple was being repaired? book of law

Older Children: What did Josiah do when the book was read to him? tore clothes

Older Children: To whom did he send men to inquire of the Lord? the prophetess Huldah

Older Children: What did Huldah tell him? punishment would come, but not in his time

Older Children: What reforms did Josiah carry out? destroyed idols, high places, groves, altars

Older Children: What did he destroy which no king before him had been able to touch? altar at Bethel

Younger Children: What great feast was celebrated when the country had been set in order? Passover

Older Children: How did Josiah die? killed by king of Egypt

Older Children: What happened to Judah afterward? conquered by Babylon

Younger Teens: What do (1) Israel, and (2) Judah, represent? (1) mind or thoughts, (2) heart or feelings

Younger Teens: What does the temple represent in us? our character

Older Teens: What is pictured by the fact that some of the kings of Judah were good? heart still has times of repentance and reform

Older Teens: Why could Judah not long survive the fall of Israel? when thoughts are captivated by worldliness, good intentions cannot long survive
