
=== Suggested Questions on the Lesson

Junior: In how many campaigns did Joshua conquer the Holy Land? three

Junior: What was the order of these campaigns? center, south, north

Junior: What familiar stories are connected with the first? Jericho, Ai, Gibeon

Junior: Were all the enemies in the land conquered? no

Primary: Where was the tabernacle set up permanently? Shiloh

Primary: What does Shiloh mean? peace

Junior: Who marked out the divisions of the land? three men from each tribe

Primary: How was it decided which tribe should have which division? by casting lots

Primary: When lots are cast, who really makes the decision? the Lord

Junior: What tribes had the center of the land? Joseph, Benjamin, Dan

Junior: Why were there two tribes of Joseph? Joseph's sons were "adopted" by Jacob

Junior: What tribes had the cross—Jordan country? Reuben, Gad, half Manasseh

Junior: What tribes had the southern part of the land? Judah, Simeon

Junior: What city was given to Caleb? Hebron

Junior: Why were the Levites not given one of the divisions of the land? in charge of worship

Primary: What were they given instead? forty-eight cities

Intermediate: What do the twelve tribes represent? all our mental faculties

Senior: What is pictured by the setting up of the tabernacle at Shiloh? putting worship in a central and settled (i.e., peaceful) part of our lives

== DEBORAH AND BARAK — Judges 4

In all classes some time should be spent on the accomplishments under Joshua and the character of the period of the Judges. Even the little children are not too young for the lesson that we never "know enough" to do right without constantly learning new truth from the Word.
