
=== Suggested Questions on the Lesson

Junior: How did Joseph get into prison in Egypt? Potiphar's wife's lies

Primary: What did he do there for the king's butler and baker? interpreted dreams

Primary: Why did Pharaoh send for him? butler remembered him when Pharaoh dreamed

Junior: What were Pharaoh's two dreams? seven fat/thin cattle, seven fat/thin ears of grain

Junior: What did Joseph tell him they meant? seven years plenty/famine

Junior: What did he advise Pharaoh to do? appoint wise leader, store food

Primary: Whom did Pharaoh choose to carry out this advice? Joseph

Junior: What powers did he give Joseph? control of whole country

Junior: Whom did he give him for his wife? Asenath

Junior: who were Joseph's two sons? Manasseh and Ephraim

Junior: How did Joseph prepare for the years of famine? stored grain all through land

Senior: What does Joseph represent? our religious sensitivity

Intermediate: What does Egypt represent? state of acquiring memory-knowledge

Intermediate: What is meant by the famine? "too busy" to learn new truth

Senior: What is pictured by Joseph's being made ruler of Egypt? putting our everyday life under control of spiritual principles

== JOSEPH'S SONS — Genesis 48; 50:22-26

Here again we have a lesson in which a familiar story needs to be reviewed briefly by way of introduction — the whole story of Joseph. In the notes for each age group the attempt has been made to suggest the particular features of the story best suited to lead into the lesson for the day with children of that age.
