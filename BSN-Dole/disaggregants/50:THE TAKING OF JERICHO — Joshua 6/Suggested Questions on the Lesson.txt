
=== Suggested Questions on the Lesson

Younger Children: What river separated the Israelites from the Holy Land? Jordan

Older Children: How did they cross it? miracle

Older Children: Where was their first camp in the Holy Land? Gilgal

Younger Children: What feast did they celebrate there? Passover

Older Children: What change was made in their food? manna ceased

Younger Children: What was the first strong city they had to conquer? Jericho

Younger Children: What did the Lord tell them to carry around the city? the ark

Younger Children: How many priests were to go before the ark? seven

Older Children: What were they to carry? ram's horn trumpets

Younger Children: How many times were they to march around the city each day for six days? once

Younger Children: How many times were they to march around it on the seventh day? seven

Younger Children: What happened when they finally blew the trumpets and shouted? walls fell

Older Children: What did they save out of the city? Rahab and family, all metals

Older Children: What did they do with everything else in it? destroyed it

Older Children: What curse was pronounced against Jericho? rebuilder will lose two sons

Younger Teens: What does entrance into the Holy Land represent? beginning regenerate life

Older Teens: What does Jericho represent? natural self-confidence

Older Teens: Why did the Lord overcome Jericho in this way? to show that such tendencies can only be conquered by the Lord's power
