
=== Suggested Questions on the Lesson

Primary: Why did Joseph's brothers go to Egypt? famine

Primary: What did Joseph do for his family? fed, housed

Primary: What two sons were born to Joseph in Egypt? Manasseh, Ephraim

Primary: Which was the older? Manasseh

Primary: When Jacob was about to die, why did Joseph bring his sons to him? blessing

Primary: Which son did Joseph expect to receive the better blessing? Manasseh

Primary: To which one did Jacob give it? Ephraim

Intermediate: What special honor did Jacob give to Joseph's sons? "adopted" them 

Junior: Where was Jacob buried? cave of Machpelah

Junior: Who else had been buried there? Abraham, Sarah, Isaac, Rebekah, Leah

Junior: When Joseph died, what did he make his family promise? take bones to Canaan

Junior: How did they preserve his body? embalming

Intermediate: What do Manasseh and Ephraim represent? Ephraim = knowledge of truth, and Manasseh = desire to live according to it

Senior: Why did Jacob give the younger son the first blessing? our continuing need first to learn what is right

The book of Genesis ends with the death of Joseph. At that time the Israelites were settled comfortably in Egypt and still respected because of Joseph. But as the years passed, and the Hebrews grew in numbers, the Egyptians began to resent them until one of the Pharaohs ordered that all male children born to the Hebrews were to be killed. This is where the story begins in the book of Exodus.
