
=== Suggested Questions on the Lesson

*Q:* (Younger Children): What is today called? +
*A:* Palm Sunday

*Q:* (Older Children): What does the day commemorate? +
*A:* the Lord's triumphal entry into Jerusalem

*Q:* (Younger Children): What did the Lord ride when He entered Jerusalem? +
*A:* a donkey

*Q:* (Younger Children): What did the disciples put on the donkey? +
*A:* their garments

*Q:* (Younger Children): What did the people strew before the Lord as He rode? +
*A:* palm branches and garments

*Q:* (Younger Children): What did the multitude cry? +
*A:* Hosanna!

*Q:* (Older Children): Where did the Lord go when He entered Jerusalem? +
*A:* the temple

*Q:* (Older Children): What did He do there? +
*A:* drove out money—changers and dove—sellers

*Q:* (Older Children): What did He say about the temple? +
*A:* it was to be a house of prayer

*Q:* (Older Children): What else did He do in the temple? +
*A:* healed the blind and the lame

*Q:* (Older Children): Where did He go to spend the night? +
*A:* Bethany

*Q:* (Older Children): Who lived there? +
*A:* Mary, Martha, and Lazarus

*Q:* (Younger Teens): What is pictured by the Lord's riding into Jerusalem on a donkey? +
*A:* that He alone had brought His natural reason under divine control

*Q:* (Younger Teens): What do the palm branches picture? +
*A:* that the Lord alone can save

*Q:* (Older Teens): What does it mean in our lives to accept the Lord as king? +
*A:* to acknowledge that His laws rule our lives and are to be obeyed
