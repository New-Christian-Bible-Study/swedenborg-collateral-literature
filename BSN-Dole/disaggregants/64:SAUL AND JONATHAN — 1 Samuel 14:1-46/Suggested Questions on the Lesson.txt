
=== Suggested Questions on the Lesson

Younger Children: Who was the first king? Saul

Younger Children: Why did the people like him? tall, strong

Older Children: What enemy did he first conquer? Ammonites

Older Children: What weaknesses did Saul have? impatient, disobedient, lacked faith

Older Children: With what enemy did he have trouble? Philistines

Younger Children: Who was Saul's son? Jonathan

Younger Children: How was he different from his father? trusted the Lord

Older Children: What did Jonathan and his armor-bearer decide to do? attack Philistine camp

Younger Children: Why did Jonathan think two men could win a victory over so many? faith

Older Children: In what kind of place did they face the enemy? rocky

Older Children: What sign did Jonathan ask of the Lord? If they say, "Come ... "

Younger Children: What happened in the Philistine army after Jonathan's victory? panic

Older Children: What order did Saul give to his men when they started after the Philistines? no food

Older Children: What did Jonathan do? ate some honey

Older Children: What did Saul try to do to him? kill him

Older Children: Who saved Jonathan? people

Older Children: What does a king represent? ruling principle

Older Teens: What does Saul represent? superficial idea of truth

Older Teens: What does Jonathan represent? knowledge from experience

Older Teens: What is pictured by the Philistines' being thrown into confusion? arguments which support self-pride conflict with themselves
