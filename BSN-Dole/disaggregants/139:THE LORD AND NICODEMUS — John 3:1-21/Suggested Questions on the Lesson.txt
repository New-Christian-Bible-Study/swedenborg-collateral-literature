
=== Suggested Questions on the Lesson

Older Children: Who were the (1) scribes, and (2) Pharisees? (1) men who copied the Scriptures, (2) a strict religious sect

Older Children: What had they done to the Scriptures? added many of their own ideas

Older Children: What did the Lord call them? hypocrites

Older Children: Why were they opposed to the Lord? He exposed their hypocrisy

Younger Children: What is the name of the man in our lesson for today? Nicodemus

Older Children: What was he besides being a Pharisee? a ruler (member of Sanhedrin)

Younger Children: Why did he come to the Lord by night? afraid of ridicule

Older Children: Why did he believe in the Lord? miracles

Older Children: What did the Lord see he wanted to ask? way to heaven

Younger Children: What did the Lord tell him? you must be born again

Younger Teens: What did the Lord mean by being "born again"? repent, follow His teachings

Older Children: What did the Lord say about the brazen serpent in the wilderness? ... so must Son of man be lifted up

Older Children: Why did the Lord say He came into the world? to save it

Older Children: Why does He say some people are condemned? will not believe

Older Children: Why should we stop to think every time we find ourselves trying to cover up our thoughts or feelings or actions? probably means we just want our own way

Younger Teens: What does it mean to be "born of water"? outer life cleansed

Older Teens: What does it mean to be "born of the Spirit"? inner life purified

Older Teens: How is this related to the work of (1) John the Baptist, and (2) the Lord? (1) cleansing outer life (2) purifying thoughts, replacing self-love with love of the Lord and the neighbor
