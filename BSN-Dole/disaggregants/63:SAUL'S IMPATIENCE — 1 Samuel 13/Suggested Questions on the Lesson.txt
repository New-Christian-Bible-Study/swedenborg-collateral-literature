
=== Suggested Questions on the Lesson

Primary: Why did the people want a king? to lead them in war

Primary: Whom did the Lord choose as the first king? Saul

Intermediate: How did the people know that the Lord had chosen Saul? drew lots

Primary: Why were they pleased to have Saul as their king? tall, strong

Junior: What enemy did Saul first conquer? Ammonites

Primary: What enemy then attacked them? Philistines

Junior: What was the relation at this time between the Philistines and Israel? Philistines in power

Primary: What had made the Philistines angry? Jonathan's attack at Geba

Junior: How were the Philistines trying to keep Israel from making war? allowed them no blacksmiths

Junior: Where was Saul's army gathered? Gilgal

Primary: What had Samuel told Saul to do? wait for him seven days

Primary: What did Saul do that was wrong? offered sacrifice himself

Junior: What excuse did he give Samuel? people were scattering

Junior: What did Samuel tell him? he would lose kingdom

Intermediate: To what do the kings correspond? divine truth ruling

Senior: To what particularly does Saul correspond? natural understanding of truth

Senior: What is the correspondence of the Philistines? faith alone

Intermediate: What were Saul's weaknesses? impatience, lack of faith
