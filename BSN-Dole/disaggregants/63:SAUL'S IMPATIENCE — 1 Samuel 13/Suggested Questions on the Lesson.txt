
=== Suggested Questions on the Lesson

Younger Children: Why did the people want a king? to lead them in war

Younger Children: Whom did the Lord choose as the first king? Saul

Younger Teens: How did the people know that the Lord had chosen Saul? drew lots

Younger Children: Why were they pleased to have Saul as their king? tall, strong

Older Children: What enemy did Saul first conquer? Ammonites

Younger Children: What enemy then attacked them? Philistines

Older Children: What was the relation at this time between the Philistines and Israel? Philistines in power

Younger Children: What had made the Philistines angry? Jonathan's attack at Geba

Older Children: How were the Philistines trying to keep Israel from making war? allowed them no blacksmiths

Older Children: Where was Saul's army gathered? Gilgal

Younger Children: What had Samuel told Saul to do? wait for him seven days

Younger Children: What did Saul do that was wrong? offered sacrifice himself

Older Children: What excuse did he give Samuel? people were scattering

Older Children: What did Samuel tell him? he would lose kingdom

Younger Teens: To what do the kings correspond? divine truth ruling

Older Teens: To what particularly does Saul correspond? natural understanding of truth

Older Teens: What is the correspondence of the Philistines? faith alone

Younger Teens: What were Saul's weaknesses? impatience, lack of faith
