
=== Suggested Questions on the Lesson

*Q:* (Older Children): Who was Jonah? +
*A:* a prophet of Israel

*Q:* (Older Children): Why did he try to run away from the Lord? +
*A:* too proud to warn Nineveh

*Q:* (Younger Children): What happened to him? +
*A:* swallowed by great fish

*Q:* (Older Children): What prophecy did he make to the Ninevites? +
*A:* doom in forty days

*Q:* (Older Children): Why was it not fulfilled? +
*A:* people repented

*Q:* (Younger Children): How did Jonah feel about it? +
*A:* angry

*Q:* (Older Children): What happened to him which pictured his state of heart and mind? +
*A:* story of gourd

*Q:* (Older Children): What lesson did the Lord draw from it for him? +
*A:* should love all people

*Q:* (Older Children): When the Lord came into the world, what had the scribes and Pharisees done with the Word? +
*A:* added their own ideas

Older Children: Can you repeat the first verse of the Gospel of John?

*Q:* (Younger Teens): What do we mean when we call the inspired Scriptures the "Word of God"? +
*A:* the written form of divine truth

*Q:* (Younger Children): Why do we always have the Word on the altar in church and open it at the beginning of the service and close it at the end? +
*A:* symbol of the Lord's presence

*Q:* (Older Children): How was the Word made flesh? +
*A:* in the person of Jesus Christ

*Q:* (Younger Children): In our chapter who testified to the fact that Jesus was the Messiah? +
*A:* John the Baptist

*Q:* (Younger Children): What sign had he been given? +
*A:* dove

*Q:* (Older Children): Which of the apostles are named in this chapter? +
*A:* Andrew, Peter, Philip, Nathanael

*Q:* (Older Children): By what other name is Nathanael elsewhere called? +
*A:* Bartholomew

*Q:* (Younger Teens): What is meant by saying that the Lord is the light of the world? +
*A:* He enlightens men's minds

*Q:* (Older Teens): What is meant by "the Son of God"? +
*A:* the Lord's visible divine humanity

*Q:* (Older Teens): Why did everyone not receive the Lord? +
*A:* some loved darkness
