
=== Suggested Questions on the Lesson

*Q:* (Younger Children): Who led a rebellion against Moses and Aaron? +
*A:* Korah

*Q:* (Younger Children): What tribe was Korah from? +
*A:* Levi

*Q:* (Younger Teens): What was the office of the Levites? +
*A:* care of tabernacle and worship

*Q:* (Younger Teens): What was Aaron's office? +
*A:* high priest

*Q:* (Younger Teens): Why was Korah dissatisfied? +
*A:* jealous of Aaron

*Q:* (Younger Children): What other men joined in the rebellion? +
*A:* Dathan and Abiram

*Q:* (Younger Teens): What tribe were they from? +
*A:* Reuben

*Q:* (Younger Children): Where did they insist on staying? +
*A:* in their own tents

*Q:* (Younger Children): How were Dathan and Abiram and their families destroyed? +
*A:* earth opened

*Q:* (Younger Teens): What other group of Korah's supporters went to the tabernacle with him? +
*A:* 250 princes of assembly

*Q:* (Younger Children): What happened to them? +
*A:* destroyed by fire

*Q:* (Older Children): What became of their censers? +
*A:* made into plates to cover altar

*Q:* (Younger Teens): What happened to the people who still murmured? +
*A:* plague

*Q:* (Younger Teens): How was the plague stopped? +
*A:* Aaron with censer

*Q:* (Younger Teens): What is pictured by the punishment of Dathan and Abiram? +
*A:* result of "faith alone"

*Q:* (Younger Teens): What does a censer represent? +
*A:* worship

*Q:* (Older Teens): What is pictured by the punishment of Korah and the princes with him? +
*A:* result of twisting truth to support one's selfish desires

*Q:* (Older Teens): Why were the censers not destroyed? +
*A:* fault not with worship as such but with wrong attitude toward it

46. Make your worship real, removing evil of pretended worship.

47. Accept truth of this need and you will see that mere outward devotion is destructive.

48. Make a clear separation in your mind between dead and live worship, thus stopping further loss of spiritual values.

49. Soberly count the losses of your foolish behavior.

50. Then return to true worship of God.
