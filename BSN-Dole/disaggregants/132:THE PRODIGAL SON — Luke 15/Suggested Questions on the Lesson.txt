
=== Suggested Questions on the Lesson

Intermediate: To whom was the parable of the great supper spoken? Pharisees

Primary: Who were the Pharisees? strict religious sect

Intermediate: Why did the Lord find fault with them? their pride

Primary: In the parable what guests were first invited? friends

Primary: What excuses did they give? field, oxen, wife

Intermediate: What did these excuses show? selfish thoughtlessness

Primary: Who were the guests who ate the supper? poor, maimed, halt, blind

Intermediate: In our chapter today who came to hear the Lord? tax collectors, sinners

Junior: What did the Pharisees and scribes say? murmured against Him

Primary: How many parables did the Lord tell them? three

Primary: What was similar about all these parables? something lost, then found

Primary: In the first parable what was lost? sheep

Primary: What was lost in the second? coin

Primary: What was lost in the third? son

Junior: What does prodigal mean? wasteful

Junior: What did the younger son waste? money

Primary: Where did he go? far country

Primary: What happened to him? he became poor and hungry

Primary: What did he decide to do? go home and serve his father

Junior: How did his father receive him? joyfully

Junior: How did his elder brother feel about this? he was jealous

Junior: What did the father answer? all I have is yours

Intermediate: What are pictured by the lost sheep and the lost coin? lost affection, lost thought

Senior: What is the inheritance which the prodigal son wasted? all our abilities

Senior: What is pictured by his arising and going back to his father? humbling self, correcting ways, trying again
