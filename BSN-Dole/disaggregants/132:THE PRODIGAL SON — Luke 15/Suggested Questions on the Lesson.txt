
=== Suggested Questions on the Lesson

Younger Teens: To whom was the parable of the great supper spoken? Pharisees

Younger Children: Who were the Pharisees? strict religious sect

Younger Teens: Why did the Lord find fault with them? their pride

Younger Children: In the parable what guests were first invited? friends

Younger Children: What excuses did they give? field, oxen, wife

Younger Teens: What did these excuses show? selfish thoughtlessness

Younger Children: Who were the guests who ate the supper? poor, maimed, halt, blind

Younger Teens: In our chapter today who came to hear the Lord? tax collectors, sinners

Older Children: What did the Pharisees and scribes say? murmured against Him

Younger Children: How many parables did the Lord tell them? three

Younger Children: What was similar about all these parables? something lost, then found

Younger Children: In the first parable what was lost? sheep

Younger Children: What was lost in the second? coin

Younger Children: What was lost in the third? son

Older Children: What does prodigal mean? wasteful

Older Children: What did the younger son waste? money

Younger Children: Where did he go? far country

Younger Children: What happened to him? he became poor and hungry

Younger Children: What did he decide to do? go home and serve his father

Older Children: How did his father receive him? joyfully

Older Children: How did his elder brother feel about this? he was jealous

Older Children: What did the father answer? all I have is yours

Younger Teens: What are pictured by the lost sheep and the lost coin? lost affection, lost thought

Older Teens: What is the inheritance which the prodigal son wasted? all our abilities

Older Teens: What is pictured by his arising and going back to his father? humbling self, correcting ways, trying again
