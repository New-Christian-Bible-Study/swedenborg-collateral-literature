
=== Suggested Questions on the Lesson

Younger Children: What did the spies bring back from the Holy Land? fruit

Younger Children: What good things did they report about the land? fertile, beautiful

Younger Children: What bad things did they report? giants, walled cities

Older Children: What did ten of the spies advise? "We are not able ... "

Older Children: What did the other two say? "We are well able ... "

Younger Children: Who were the two spies who trusted in the Lord? Caleb, Joshua

Older Children: Which advice did the people take? that of the ten

Older Children: What was the result? 38 years more in desert

Older Children: How long altogether did they wander in the wilderness? 40 years

Older Children: What was their last encampment before they entered the Holy Land? Plain of Moab

Younger Children: When Moses died, who became the leader of the Israelites? Joshua

Older Children: What do you remember about Joshua from earlier lessons? good spy, army leader

Older Children: What was the Lord's charge to Joshua? "Be strong and of a good courage"

Older Children: What tribes were given homes east of the Jordan? Reuben, Gad, half Manasseh

Older Children: What were the men of these tribes to do before they settled down? help others fight

Younger Teens: What does entering the Holy Land mean? beginning regeneration

Older Teens: What is the difference in correspondence between (1) Moses, and (2) Joshua? (1) law obeyed
(2) truth fighting

Older Teens: What is the difference between (1) reformation, and (2) regeneration? (1) putting outer life in order, and (2) getting a new will from the Lord
