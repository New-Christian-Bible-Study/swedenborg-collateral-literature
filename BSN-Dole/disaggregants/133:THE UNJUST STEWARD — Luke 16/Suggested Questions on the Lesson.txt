
=== Suggested Questions on the Lesson

*Q:* (Older Children): To whom did the Lord speak the parable of the unjust steward? +
*A:* His disciples

*Q:* (Younger Children): What had the steward done? +
*A:* wasted his master's goods

*Q:* (Older Children): With what was he threatened? +
*A:* loss of his job

*Q:* (Younger Children): What did he say he could not do? +
*A:* dig

*Q:* (Younger Children): What was he ashamed to do? +
*A:* beg

*Q:* (Older Children): What did he decide to do? +
*A:* take less than what debtors owed

*Q:* (Older Children): What did his lord think of the course he chose? +
*A:* commended him

*Q:* (Younger Teens): What does this parable teach us? +
*A:* recognize our shortcomings, do our best

*Q:* (Younger Children): What does the Lord say about serving two masters? +
*A:* cannot do it

*Q:* (Older Children): What is meant by mammon? +
*A:* riches, things wanted selfishly

*Q:* (Older Children): What did the Pharisees think of this parable? +
*A:* laughed at it

*Q:* (Older Children): What other parable did the Lord give them? +
*A:* rich man and Lazarus

*Q:* (Younger Children): Where did the beggar go when he died? +
*A:* Abraham's bosom (heaven)

*Q:* (Younger Children): Where did the rich man go? +
*A:* hell

*Q:* (Older Children): What did the rich man first ask Abraham to do? +
*A:* send Lazarus with water

*Q:* (Older Children): Why could not Abraham do it? +
*A:* great gulf between

*Q:* (Older Children): What did the rich man then ask? +
*A:* warn my brothers

*Q:* (Older Children): What did Abraham tell him? +
*A:* they have Moses and the prophets

*Q:* (Older Children): What did the rich man think would convince his brothers? +
*A:* if one went from the dead

*Q:* (Older Children): What did Abraham say to this? +
*A:* this would not persuade them

*Q:* (Younger Teens): What does this parable teach us? +
*A:* we decide here what we will be to eternity

*Q:* (Older Teens): How can a course of action which in the letter seems dishonest be called "wise"? +
*A:* wrong to feel oneself inherently good, unworthy to ask for "pure" mercy, thus the only course left is to serve the Lord as best we can

Older Teens: In the second parable who are meant by (1) the rich man, and (2) the beggar?
(1) those who think they are spiritually rich
(2) those who recognize their spiritual poverty and long for righteousness
