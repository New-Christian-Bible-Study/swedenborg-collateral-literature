
=== Suggested Questions on the Lesson

*Q:* (Younger Children): Who was the second of the three great kings before the division of the land? +
*A:* David

*Q:* (Older Children): What special talent did David have? +
*A:* musician and poet

*Q:* (Younger Children): What book of the Bible was written in part through David? +
*A:* Psalms

*Q:* (Older Children): What did David say about what he wrote? +
*A:* it was the Word of the Lord

*Q:* (Younger Children): What is a psalm? +
*A:* a song

*Q:* (Younger Children): How did the Jews use the Psalms? +
*A:* hymns

*Q:* (Younger Teens): What did the Lord tell His disciples about them? +
*A:* they were about Him

*Q:* (Older Children): Can you repeat the first Psalm? +
*A:* "Blessed is the man ... "

*Q:* (Younger Children): What does blessed mean? +
*A:* happy

*Q:* (Older Children): What three things are we told not to do if we want to be happy? +
*A:* walk with ungodly, stand with sinners, sit with scornful

*Q:* (Older Children): What are we told to do? +
*A:* delight in the law of the Lord

*Q:* (Younger Children): To what is a good man likened in the first Psalm? +
*A:* a tree

*Q:* (Younger Children): To what is an ungodly man likened? +
*A:* chaff

*Q:* (Younger Teens): What does the Lord do for us in the Psalms? +
*A:* expresses our spiritual states and needs

*Q:* (Older Teens): How does the first Psalm set the pattern for the whole book? +
*A:* If we wish to be happy we should delight in the law of the Lord, and meditate daily on the Word.
