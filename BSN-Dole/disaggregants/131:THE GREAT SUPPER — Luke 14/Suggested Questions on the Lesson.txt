
=== Suggested Questions on the Lesson

Younger Teens: Why did the Lord put the story of Mary and Martha in the Word immediately after the parable of the Good Samaritan? to help us learn how to love the neighbor

Older Children: Who were the Pharisees? a strict religious sect

Older Children: In our lesson for today what fault did the Lord see in them? they wanted the best for themselves

Younger Children: What parable did He give them? the great supper

Younger Children: Whom did the man in the parable first invite to his supper? friends

Older Children: What three excuses were given by his friends for not coming? field, oxen, wife

Older Children: What fault in his friends did this show? selfish thoughtlessness

Older Children: Whom did the man afterward find to enjoy his supper? poor, maimed, halt, blind

Older Children: Whom does the man in this parable represent? the Lord

Older Children: To what "feast" does the Lord invite people? His Word, containing His love and truth

Younger Children: Where is the Lord's house? the church

Older Children: Who are the friends who make excuses? all of us who call ourselves Christians

Younger Teens: What does eating at the Lord's table represent? feeding our souls with the Lord's goodness and truth

Older Teens: What do the three excuses picture?
(1) field: intellectual pride (mind)
(2) oxen: wrong priorities in our affections (heart)
(3) wife: "wedded" to selfish ways (act)
