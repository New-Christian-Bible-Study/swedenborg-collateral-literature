
=== Suggested Questions on the Lesson

Intermediate: Why did the Lord put the story of Mary and Martha in the Word immediately after the parable of the Good Samaritan? to help us learn how to love the neighbor

Junior: Who were the Pharisees? a strict religious sect

Junior: In our lesson for today what fault did the Lord see in them? they wanted the best for themselves

Primary: What parable did He give them? the great supper

Primary: Whom did the man in the parable first invite to his supper? friends

Junior: What three excuses were given by his friends for not coming? field, oxen, wife

Junior: What fault in his friends did this show? selfish thoughtlessness

Junior: Whom did the man afterward find to enjoy his supper? poor, maimed, halt, blind

Junior: Whom does the man in this parable represent? the Lord

Junior: To what "feast" does the Lord invite people? His Word, containing His love and truth

Primary: Where is the Lord's house? the church

Junior: Who are the friends who make excuses? all of us who call ourselves Christians

Junior: Who are the poor people who accept His invitation? the humble—minded

Intermediate: What does eating at the Lord's table represent? feeding our souls with the Lord's goodness and truth

Senior: What do the three excuses picture?
(1) field: intellectual pride (mind)
(2) oxen: wrong priorities in our affections (heart)
(3) wife: "wedded" to selfish ways (act)
