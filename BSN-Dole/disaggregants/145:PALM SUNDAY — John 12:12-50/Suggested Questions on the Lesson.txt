
=== Suggested Questions on the Lesson

Older Children: How long was the Lord's ministry? about three years

Older Children: Why did He frequently go down to Jerusalem? for the feast days

Younger Children: Why did He go there at the beginning of the last week of His life on earth? Passover

Younger Children: What kind of animal did He ride into Jerusalem on that day? a donkey

Older Children: Why did most of the people welcome Him? thought He would become king

Younger Children: What did they carry in their hands? palm branches

Younger Children: What did they cry? Hosanna

Older Children: What does Hosanna mean? save now

Younger Teens: Why did the Lord ride on a young donkey? sign of royalty, to fulfill prophecy (Zechariah 9:9)

Older Children: Who were angry at His coming? scribes and Pharisees

Older Children: What Gentiles were anxious to see Him? Greeks

Older Children: Which apostles were asked to introduce them to Him? Philip and Andrew

Older Children: How did the Lord explain the necessity of His death? parable of grain of wheat

Older Children: What did He say we must do to save our spiritual lives? lose our selfish life

Older Children: Why did He say the scribes and Pharisees could not see the truth? didn't want to

Older Children: Why did some who did believe Him not support Him openly? loved praises of men ...

Younger Teens: What is it that judges us? divine truth

Older Teens: What is represented by the Lord's riding on a young donkey? His mastery of inclination to judge by worldly reasoning or outward appearance

Older Teens: What is represented by the palm branches? the divine truth of the letter of the Word
