
=== Suggested Questions on the Lesson

Primary: What does the Lord call Himself in this lesson? Good Shepherd

Primary: What does He say a true shepherd does? calls his sheep by name

Primary: What does He say about the sheep? they know His voice

Junior: What does He say about those who came before Him? thieves and robbers

Primary: What else does He call Himself, besides Shepherd? door of fold

Junior: How is He the door? through Him we enter heaven

Junior: What does He say about the hireling? cares nothing for the sheep

Junior: What does He say about other sheep He has? not of this fold

Junior: What did some of the Jews ask Him to tell them? are you the Christ?

Junior: What did He point to as witness that He came from God? His works

Junior: Why did He say they would not believe Him? not His sheep

Junior: What did He say about Himself and the Father? I and Father are one

Junior: What did they then accuse Him of? blasphemy

Primary: What other very familiar Bible passage calls the Lord a Shepherd? twenty-third Psalm

Intermediate: What do (1) lambs, and (2) sheep represent? (1) the quality of innocence out of which grows (2) the quality of genuine goodness in the life

Senior: What is innocence, spiritually understood? the acknowledgment that all goodness and wisdom are from the Lord; thus the willingness to be led by the Lord (literally, harmlessness]
