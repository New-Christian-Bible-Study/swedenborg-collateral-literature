
=== Suggested Questions on the Lesson

Younger Children: How long did the captivity in Babylon last? about seventy years

Younger Children: What king permitted the people to go back to the Holy Land? Cyrus of Persia

Younger Children: What did he tell them to do? rebuild the temple

Older Children: Who was their leader? Zerubabbel

Younger Children: Did all the people want to go back? no

Younger Children: What were those who remained in Babylon told to do? give money and supplies

Older Children: Why did those who returned interrupt the building of the temple? more interested in their own comfort

Younger Children: What did they build instead? houses for themselves

Younger Children: How did the Lord rebuke them? through prophets (Haggai and Zechariah)

Older Children: In our chapter how did Zechariah's vision come to him? from an angel

Older Children: What did he see in the vision? lampstand and two olive trees

Older Children: What message was given him for Zerubbabel? "not by might, nor by power, but by my spirit"

Older Children: What did the angel tell him the lamps were? the eyes of the Lord

Younger Teens: What were the two olive trees? two anointed ones (love of Lord and neighbor)

Younger Teens: What does the temple represent? a heavenly character, or the church in us

Younger Teens: What is pictured by the lampstand? the Word, containing divine truth

Older Teens: What are the "eyes of the Lord"? His divine providence

Older Teens: What do the olive trees picture? the good of love to the Lord and love to the neighbor
