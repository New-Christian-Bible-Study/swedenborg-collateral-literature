
=== Suggested Questions on the Lesson

Primary: What is a prophet? one who speaks for the Lord

Primary: About what great prophet to Israel have we had a story? Elijah

Junior: What Judge was also a prophet? Samuel

Junior: Into what three parts did the Hebrews divide their Bible? Law, Prophets, Writings

Junior: What books did they include in their Prophets which we think of as historical books? Joshua, Judges, Samuel, Kings

Junior: Into what two groups do we divide the Prophets? Major, Minor

Junior: Why are they called Major and Minor? longer, shorter

Primary: How many Major Prophets are there? four

Primary: Can you name them? Isaiah, Jeremiah, Ezekiel, Daniel

Primary: About which one is our lesson today? Isaiah

Junior: When and where did Isaiah prophesy? Judah, reigns of Uzziah, Jotham, Ahaz, Hezekiah

Primary: Whom did Isaiah see in his vision? the Lord

Primary: What can you tell about the seraphim? six wings

Primary: Why was Isaiah frightened? "unclean" lips

Junior: How did the Lord purify Isaiah's lips? live coal

Primary: What did the Lord ask? "Whom shall I send?"

Primary: What did Isaiah say? "Here am I; send me."

Intermediate: What was the mission of the prophets? point out evils, call to repentance

Senior: What do we learn in this lesson are the qualities which we must have if we are to serve the Lord effectively? vision of Lord, humility, consecration, willingness to serve

This is another background lesson and the important thing, except for Primaries, is to see that the classes know who are meant by the Major Prophets, where their books come in the Bible, what their position was with reference to the history concerned, and what the function of a prophet was. Also essential are the meaning of the word prophet, the fact that the prophets spoke by inspiration, and something of the various types of inspiration.
