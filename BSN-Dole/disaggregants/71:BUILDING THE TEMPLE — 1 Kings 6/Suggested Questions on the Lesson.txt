
=== Suggested Questions on the Lesson

Older Children: What was David's principal work? conquering enemies

Older Children: Why could he not build the temple? needs time of peace

Younger Children: Who did build it? Solomon

Younger Children: What does the name Solomon mean? peace

Older Children: Who furnished some of the materials for the temple? Hiram

Older Children: How did Solomon pay him? grain, oil, cities

Younger Children: What were the principal materials? stone, cedar, gold

Older Children: How was the temple like the tabernacle? same general plan

Older Children: How was it different? more elaborate, permanent

Older Children: Where in Jerusalem was it built? Mount Moriah

Older Children: Why was it built more quietly than most buildings? stone cut and shaped at quarry

Younger Children: How long did it take to build it? seven years

Younger Teens: What are pictured by (1) Saul, (2) David, and (3) Solomon? obeying truth from (1) duty, (2) reverence, and (3) love

Older Teens: What is meant in our lives by David's not being permitted to build the temple? character takes final form only after temptation battles

Older Teens: What is meant in our lives by the 'temple materials' being prepared before they were brought to Jerusalem? forming character by right actions in outer, everyday life
