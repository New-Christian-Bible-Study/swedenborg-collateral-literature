
=== Suggested Questions on the Lesson

*Q:* (Older Children): What was David's principal work? +
*A:* conquering enemies

*Q:* (Older Children): Why could he not build the temple? +
*A:* needs time of peace

*Q:* (Younger Children): Who did build it? +
*A:* Solomon

*Q:* (Younger Children): What does the name Solomon mean? +
*A:* peace

*Q:* (Older Children): Who furnished some of the materials for the temple? +
*A:* Hiram

*Q:* (Older Children): How did Solomon pay him? +
*A:* grain, oil, cities

*Q:* (Younger Children): What were the principal materials? +
*A:* stone, cedar, gold

*Q:* (Older Children): How was the temple like the tabernacle? +
*A:* same general plan

*Q:* (Older Children): How was it different? +
*A:* more elaborate, permanent

*Q:* (Older Children): Where in Jerusalem was it built? +
*A:* Mount Moriah

*Q:* (Older Children): Why was it built more quietly than most buildings? +
*A:* stone cut and shaped at quarry

*Q:* (Younger Children): How long did it take to build it? +
*A:* seven years

*Q:* (Younger Teens): What are pictured by (1) Saul, (2) David, and (3) Solomon? +
*A:* obeying truth from (1) duty, (2) reverence, and (3) love

*Q:* (Older Teens): What is meant in our lives by David's not being permitted to build the temple? +
*A:* character takes final form only after temptation battles

*Q:* (Older Teens): What is meant in our lives by the 'temple materials' being prepared before they were brought to Jerusalem? +
*A:* forming character by right actions in outer, everyday life
