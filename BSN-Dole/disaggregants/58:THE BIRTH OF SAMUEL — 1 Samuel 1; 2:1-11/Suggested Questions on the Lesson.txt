
=== Suggested Questions on the Lesson

*Q:* (Younger Teens): Why do we not study the book of Ruth? +
*A:* no inner sense

*Q:* (Younger Children): Who was the last of the Judges? +
*A:* Samuel

*Q:* (Older Children): Who were Samuel's parents? +
*A:* Elkanah and Hannah

*Q:* (Older Children): Where was the tabernacle at this time? +
*A:* Shiloh

*Q:* (Younger Children): Who was the high priest? +
*A:* Eli

*Q:* (Younger Children): What promise did Hannah make? +
*A:* if she had a son, to give him to the Lord

*Q:* (Older Children): How did she keep it? +
*A:* she brought Samuel to live with Eli

*Q:* (Younger Children): Where did Samuel grow up? +
*A:* at Shiloh

*Q:* (Younger Teens): Why did the people get into so much trouble in the time of the Judges? +
*A:* they forgot the Lord

*Q:* (Older Teens): What does Samuel represent in us? +
*A:* the Lord's Word to us through our childhood remains


It is important that the children see the fallacy of thinking that we can lead and govern ourselves apart from regular worship of the Lord and obedience to Him. The sin of Eli's sons shows how even worship can be degraded by selfishness, self-satisfaction, and disobedience.
