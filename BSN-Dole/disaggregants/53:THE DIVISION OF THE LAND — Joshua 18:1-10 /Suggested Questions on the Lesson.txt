
=== Suggested Questions on the Lesson

Junior: In how many campaigns did Joshua conquer the Holy Land? + 
_three_

Junior: What was the order of these campaigns? + 
_center, south, north_

Junior: What familiar stories are connected with the first? + 
_Jericho, Ai, Gibeon_

Junior: Were all the enemies in the land conquered? + 
_no_

Primary: Where was the tabernacle set up permanently? + 
_Shiloh_

Primary: What does Shiloh mean? + 
_peace_

Junior: Who marked out the divisions of the land? + 
_three men from each tribe_

Primary: How was it decided which tribe should have which division? + 
_by casting lots_

Primary: When lots are cast, who really makes the decision? + 
_the Lord_

Junior: What tribes had the center of the land? + 
_Joseph, Benjamin, Dan_

Junior: Why were there two tribes of Joseph? + 
_Joseph's sons were "adopted" by Jacob_

Junior: What tribes had the crossâ€”Jordan country? + 
_Reuben, Gad, half Manasseh_

Junior: What tribes had the southern part of the land? + 
_Judah, Simeon_

Junior: What city was given to Caleb? + 
_Hebron_

Junior: Why were the Levites not given one of the divisions of the land? + 
_in charge of worship_

Primary: What were they given instead? + 
_forty-eight cities_

Intermediate: What do the twelve tribes represent? + 
_all our mental faculties_

Senior: What is pictured by the setting up of the tabernacle at Shiloh? + 
_putting worship in a central and settled (i.e., peaceful) part of our lives_
