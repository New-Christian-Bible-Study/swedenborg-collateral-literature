
=== Suggested Questions on the Lesson

Older Children: In how many campaigns did Joshua conquer the Holy Land? +
_three_

Older Children: What was the order of these campaigns? +
_center, south, north_

Older Children: What familiar stories are connected with the first? +
_Jericho, Ai, Gibeon_

Older Children: Were all the enemies in the land conquered? +
_no_

Younger Children: Where was the tabernacle set up permanently? +
_Shiloh_

Younger Children: What does Shiloh mean? +
_peace_

Older Children: Who marked out the divisions of the land? +
_three men from each tribe_

Younger Children: How was it decided which tribe should have which division? +
_by casting lots_

Younger Children: When lots are cast, who really makes the decision? +
_the Lord_

Older Children: What tribes had the center of the land? +
_Joseph, Benjamin, Dan_

Older Children: Why were there two tribes of Joseph? +
_Joseph's sons were "adopted" by Jacob_

Older Children: What tribes had the crossâ€”Jordan country? +
_Reuben, Gad, half Manasseh_

Older Children: What tribes had the southern part of the land? +
_Judah, Simeon_

Older Children: What city was given to Caleb? +
_Hebron_

Older Children: Why were the Levites not given one of the divisions of the land? +
_in charge of worship_

Younger Children: What were they given instead? +
_forty-eight cities_

Younger Teens: What do the twelve tribes represent? +
_all our mental faculties_

Older Teens: What is pictured by the setting up of the tabernacle at Shiloh? +
_putting worship in a central and settled (i.e., peaceful) part of our lives_
