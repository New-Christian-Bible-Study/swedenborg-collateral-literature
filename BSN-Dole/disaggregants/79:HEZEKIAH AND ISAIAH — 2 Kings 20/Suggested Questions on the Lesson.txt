
=== Suggested Questions on the Lesson

Primary: What king of Judah is our lesson about today? Hezekiah

Junior: What happened to Israel early in his reign? captured by Assyria

Junior: How was Judah saved from Assyria? angel of the Lord

Primary: Who was the prophet in Judah in Hezekiah's time? Isaiah

Primary: When Hezekiah fell sick, what did Isaiah tell him? you will die

Primary: What did Hezekiah do? prayed

Primary: What new message from the Lord did Isaiah bring him? he would live fifteen more years

Primary: What was the matter with Hezekiah? boil (ulcer)

Primary: How did Isaiah cure him? figs

Junior: What sign did Hezekiah ask? shadow go back ten degrees

Junior: What king sent messengers to Hezekiah? Babylon

Junior: What did Hezekiah show them? all his treasures

Junior: What did Isaiah tell him? that was foolish

Intermediate: How did the captivity of Israel affect Judah? left it unprotected on north

Senior: What do (1) Assyria, and (2) Babylon, represent? (1) worldly, naturalistic thinking (2) love of dominion from love of self

Israel did not heed the warnings of Elijah and Elisha and was finally taken captive by the Assyrians. All her people were carried away captive and never came back. But in Judah, although there had been a number of wicked kings, and worship of the Lord had declined and idolatry flourished, there had also been several good kings who instituted reforms and tried to restore true worship. Assyria was not allowed to overcome Judah. This brings us to our lesson. All teachers should read chapter 23 as well as 22.
